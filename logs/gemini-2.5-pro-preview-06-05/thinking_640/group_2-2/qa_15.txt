### Step-by-Step Reasoning

**Step 1: Define and Quantify the Speed of Light**

**Step 2: Define and Quantify the Speed of Sound**

*   **What it is:** The speed of sound is the speed at which sound waves (which are mechanical vibrations) travel through a medium (like air, water, or a solid). Unlike the speed of light, it is not a constant and depends entirely on the properties of the medium it is traveling through (e.g., its temperature, density, and stiffness).
*   **Its value (in a common medium):** The speed of sound in dry air at 20°C (68°F) is approximately:
    *   **343 meters per second** (m/s)
    *   Roughly **0.343 kilometers per second** (km/s)
    *   Roughly **0.213 miles per second** (mi/s)

*   **Speed of Light:** 299,792,458 m/s
*   **Speed of Sound (in air):** 343 m/s

299,792,458 / 343 ≈ 874,030

This calculation shows that the speed of light is over **870,000 times faster** than the speed of sound in air.
