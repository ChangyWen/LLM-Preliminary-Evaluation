### Step-by-Step Reasoning

**Step 1: Understand the pH Scale**

The pH scale measures how acidic or basic a substance is. The scale ranges from 0 to 14.
*   A pH of **7** is considered **neutral**.
*   A pH **less than 7** is considered **acidic**.
*   A pH **greater than 7** is considered **basic** (or alkaline).

The lower the pH value, the stronger the acidity.

**Step 2: Determine the pH of Water**

Pure water is the benchmark for neutrality on the pH scale. In pure water, the concentration of hydrogen ions (H⁺) and hydroxide ions (OH⁻) is perfectly balanced.
*   The pH of pure water is **7**.

**Step 3: Determine the pH of Lemon Juice**

Lemon juice is sour because it contains acids, primarily **citric acid** and some ascorbic acid (Vitamin C). When these acids dissolve in water, they release a high concentration of hydrogen ions (H⁺) into the solution.
*   A high concentration of hydrogen ions makes a substance acidic.
*   The pH of lemon juice is typically between **2 and 3**.

**Step 4: Compare the pH Values**

Now, we compare the pH value of lemon juice to the pH value of water.
*   pH of Lemon Juice: ~2 to 3
*   pH of Water: ~7

The number 2 (or 3) is significantly lower than the number 7.

### Final Answer

**Yes.**

The pH of lemon juice (around 2-3) is lower than the pH of water (7), because lemon juice is an acidic substance.
