### Step-by-Step Reasoning

The acceleration due to gravity on the surface of a celestial body is determined by two main factors: its **mass** and its **radius**. The relationship is described by Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation.

**Step 1: The Governing Formula**

The formula for acceleration due to gravity ($g$) on the surface of a spherical body is:

$g = G * M / r^2$

Where:
*   $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity.
*   $G$ is the universal gravitational constant (it's the same value everywhere in the universe).
*   $M$ is the **mass** of the celestial body (like the Earth or the Moon).
*   $r$ is the **radius** of the celestial body.

This formula tells us that gravity is stronger for bodies with more mass ($M$) and weaker for bodies with a larger radius ($r$).

*   **Moon's Mass:** approximately 7.34 x 10²² kg
