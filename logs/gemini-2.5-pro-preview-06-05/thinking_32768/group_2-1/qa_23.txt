### Step-by-Step Reasoning

**Step 1: Define the pH Scale**
The pH scale measures how acidic or alkaline a substance is. The scale ranges from 0 to 14.
*   A pH of **7** is considered **neutral**.
*   A pH **less than 7** is considered **acidic**.
*   A pH **greater than 7** is considered **alkaline** (or basic).

The lower the pH number, the stronger the acidity.

**Step 2: Analyze the pH of Water**
Pure water is the standard for neutrality on the pH scale. It has an equal balance of acidic hydrogen ions (H+) and alkaline hydroxide ions (OH-). This balance gives pure water a pH of approximately **7**.

**Step 3: Analyze the Composition of Lemon Juice**
Lemon juice gets its characteristic sour taste from the acids it contains. The primary acid in lemon juice is **citric acid**, along with some ascorbic acid (Vitamin C).

**Step 4: Explain How Acids Affect pH**
When an acid is dissolved in a solution, it increases the concentration of hydrogen ions (H+). According to the definition of the pH scale, a higher concentration of hydrogen ions results in a **lower pH value**.

**Step 5: Compare Lemon Juice and Water**
*   **Water** is neutral, with a pH of ~7.
*   **Lemon juice**, being full of citric acid, has a much higher concentration of hydrogen ions than water. This high acidity places its pH value typically between **2 and 3**.

Since the number 2 or 3 is significantly lower than the number 7, the pH of lemon juice is lower than the pH of water.
