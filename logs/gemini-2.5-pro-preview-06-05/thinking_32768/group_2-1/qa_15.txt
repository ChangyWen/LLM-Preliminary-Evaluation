### Step-by-Step Reasoning

**Step 2: Define the Speed of Sound**

*   Sound is a mechanical wave, which means it needs a medium (like air, water, or a solid) to travel through. It travels by causing vibrations in the particles of that medium.
*   The speed of sound is not constant; it changes depending on the medium it's traveling through, as well as the temperature and pressure of that medium.
*   **Value:** In dry air at sea level at 20°C (68°F), the speed of sound is approximately **343 meters per second** (m/s), which is about **767 miles per hour**.
