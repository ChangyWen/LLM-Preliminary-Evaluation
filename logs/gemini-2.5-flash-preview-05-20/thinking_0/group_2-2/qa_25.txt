**Step 1: Understand the Concept of Acceleration Due to Gravity**

Acceleration due to gravity (often denoted as 'g') is the acceleration experienced by an object due to the gravitational pull of a celestial body. It depends on two main factors:

*   **Mass of the celestial body (M):** A more massive body exerts a stronger gravitational pull.
*   **Radius of the celestial body (R):** The closer an object is to the center of mass of the body, the stronger the gravitational pull.

The formula for acceleration due to gravity is:

$g = \frac{GM}{R^2}$

Where:
*   G is the universal gravitational constant ($6.674 \times 10^{-11} N \cdot m^2/kg^2$)
*   M is the mass of the celestial body
*   R is the radius of the celestial body
