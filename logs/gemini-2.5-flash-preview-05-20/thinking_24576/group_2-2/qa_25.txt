    The acceleration due to gravity ($g$) on the surface of a celestial body is given by Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation, specifically derived for the surface:

    Where:
    *   $G$ is the Universal Gravitational Constant (which is the same everywhere in the universe).
    *   $M$ is the mass of the celestial body.
    *   $R$ is the radius of the celestial body.

    From the formula, we can see that the acceleration due to gravity ($g$) depends on two main factors:
